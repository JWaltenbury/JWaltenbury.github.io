Fancy Cakes

This app is designed to demonstrate some fancy cakes I've found on the internet, including a brief explanation of what each cake is. I've also included some audio and video related to the cakes. Not that this app is focused more on the design elements of the cake and less on the actual baking; although I have included links to the recipes of each cake.

This cake displays decorating skills more than anything and uses fondant to create the lego bricks. This cake was created for a fundraiser for kids, and as such, had to have a whimsical style to it. The cake itself is a chocolate mud cake, but you could really use any type of cake for this decorating since you are just adding fondant on top.

Syrup is used to hold the fondant onto the cake, and the bricks themselves are made individually by moulding the fondant. Assemble the lego bricks like, well, lego bricks by stacking them on top of each other. The worst part of this cake is the connector dots on top are made by hand using a straw, and then added on to make it look like they are part of the brick.

The lego people are not necessary, though they are a nice touch. Rather than making them out of fondant, you could simply use real lego figures.

This is another cake where it has more to do with decorating, and less to do with the actual cake. This type of cake is currently very popular due to its glossy finish and the potential to use unique patterns. Making a cake like this isn't particularly difficult, nor does it take a lot of time. It looks fantastic though, so this can be a go-to recipe if you want to impress your friends.

The cake itself is just a white layer cake, and it has butter cream and raspberry jam for flavouring. Once you have baked the cake and iced it, you are ready for the glaze part. Note that you could use other types of cakes, icing, or filling, and they would all work well. The only consideration is your cake must have smooth edges so that the glaze works properly.

To make the glaze, you use powdered gelatin and a simple syrup. The colour comes from food colouring, and you make the base of it dark purple and drizzle on the lighter colours to give it that starry look. It is important to pour the glaze on somewhere that the excess can be caught as it falls off the cake, as you do not want it pooling around the bottom edge.

This type of cake falls under the 'Dessert Imposter' category, which means that it is designed to not look like a dessert. While this one isn't really a cake, it is made up of pound cake, so it technically counts. The filling is strawberries, Nutella, and mint leaves.

First, make a pound cake by following the recipe, then slice it into pieces of 'bread' and cut up some strawberries. Using a loaf pan is obviously the best choice here, as the cake will naturally come out in the shape of a loaf of bread. Spread on the Nutella and stack the mint and strawberries on top. That's it!

While the bread, tomato, and lettuce all work well here the issue is with the bacon. Nutella doesn't really look like bacon other than the colour, so you won't really be fooling anyone with this cake. Still, it is a fun and simple idea you could bring to any get-together!

Finally, we have the Geode cake. This cake is designed to look like a naturally occurring rock formation, and has endless possibilities on the design you can use (since geodes come in all shapes and sizes). Similarly to the Lego cake, this cake is just a basic cake covered in fondant. This means you can use any cake you want, in any flavour. The biggest consideration is that having a taller cake allows you to have larger geode designs.

The next step after covering the cake in fondant is to cut out the section you want to use for your geode. Then, fill the hole with frosting so the rock candy has something to stick to. Add some piping gel on top of the frosting, and then start pressing the rock candy into the hole. Ideally you want darker candy towards the center of the cake to give the illusion of darkness.

There is no right or wrong way to create your geode, though using similar colours of rock candy can make things look more realistic. Remember that all geodes are different and have fun decorating!
